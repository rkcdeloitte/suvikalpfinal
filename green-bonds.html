Step-By-Step Procedure for Green Bonds

Work in Progress

Green Bonds facilitate capital flows from responsible investors in developed countries into green projects in emerging economies.

Issuing a Green Bond

  1. Pre-issuance
  2. Define a green bond framework
  3. Define how project meets green bond eligibility criteria (use of proceeds)
  4. Put in place project selection process and select eligible projects (selection of projects and assets)
  5. Set up accounts and process to earmark and allocate proceeds — “ring fence” the proceeds (management of proceeds)
  6. Establish reporting processes; get pre-issuance external review (external review)

Launch the Bond into the Market

  1. Post-issuance
  2. Allocate proceeds to the projects
  3. Monitor the projects
  4. Publish impact report
  5. Post-issuance audit if necessary

1. Establish a Green Bond Framework

Create a formal document defining your project's environmental objectives. It must align with global standards like the Green Bond Principles (GBP) established by the International Capital Market Association.

2. Verify Your “Use of Proceeds”

Your project must clearly fall under eligible green categories.

  1. Renewable energy (solar, wind, hydro)
  2. Energy efficiency and green buildings
  3. Clean transportation (EVs, metro systems)
  4. Waste management, water conservation, and biodiversity

3. Obtain a Second-Party Opinion (SPO)

To prove your project avoids “greenwashing” and genuinely meets sustainability criteria, hire an independent reviewer, rating agency, or environmental consultant (e.g., Sustainalytics or Moody's) to validate your framework.

4. Issue the Bond

Work with an underwriter or investment bank to structure the debt. The bond is then issued into the market. If your project size is too small for a standard corporate bond, consider accessing funds via specialized financing facilities.

5. Track and Manage the Proceeds

You must create a segregated account to track the incoming funds. The money raised can only be disbursed to the specified eligible projects.

6. Report on Progress

You are legally required to provide regular allocation and impact reports. These documents show investors how their funds were spent and the exact environmental impact achieved (e.g., carbon emissions reduced).

To attract international and institutional investors, you must list your green bond on a recognized sustainable stock exchange or a dedicated green bond segment. The leading international platforms for listing green bonds include:

  • Luxembourg Green Exchange (LGX)

    The world's first and largest dedicated platform for sustainable securities, requiring strict compliance with green frameworks.

  • London Stock Exchange (LSE) Sustainable Bond Market

    Offers high visibility to global institutional funds and requires rigorous post-issuance reporting.

  • Euronext ESG Bonds

    Connects issuers to a massive European investor network across multiple European cities.

  • Nasdaq Sustainable Debt Market

    Highly popular for Nordic and European issuers, offering extensive visibility to ESG-focused asset managers.

  • Singapore Exchange (SGX) Sustainable Fixed Income

    The premier hub for listing green and transition bonds targeting Asian investors.

If your project is too small for a major public stock exchange listing, consider these alternative avenues:

  • Private Placement

    Sell the bonds directly to a select group of institutional investors (like pension funds or insurance companies) without a public exchange listing.

  • Sustainable Crowd-investing

    Utilize specialized green crowdfunding platforms to raise capital directly from retail investors.

  • Green Aggregators

    Partner with development banks that bundle smaller local projects into one large, exchange-tradable green bond.
