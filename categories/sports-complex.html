Final Recommendations / Sports Complex
Sports Complex
Precedents
Indoor Sports Complex, Asansol Coalfields, West Bengal
This concept proposes the development of Sports Complex Zones on closed, reclaimed, stabilized, or repurposed mine land as part of a sustainable mine-closure and post-mining land-use strategy. The objective is to convert under-utilized mine land into a community-oriented, youth-focused, health-promoting, income-supporting and socially productive land-use system centered around outdoor sports grounds, indoor sports halls, athletics tracks, multi-purpose courts, fitness zones, training academies, community recreation spaces, and sports-linked livelihood and service activities. Sports infrastructure is consistent with the objectives of the Khelo India Scheme, which promotes sustainable physical and social infrastructure using convergent funding including government schemes, CSR, public/private/community sponsorships and District Mineral Funds.
A Sports Complex is particularly suited for closed coal mine areas where the land is already under public/PSU control, where local communities require long-term welfare assets, and where mine closure must create visible socio-economic value. Unlike heavy industrial reuse, a sports complex requires comparatively low pollution-generating activity; supports community welfare and youth development; can be integrated with eco-parks, plantations, water bodies, and public recreation zones; and generates direct and indirect employment through facility management, coaching, security, events, food kiosks, equipment rental, maintenance, and sports academies. This aligns with India's mine-closure shift toward productive, community-oriented repurposing, as reflected in frameworks such as L.I.V.E.S., R.E.C.L.A.I.M. and A.R.T.H.A.
Relevant Schemes & Policy

Policy levers and frameworks supporting Sports Complex development on closed coal mine land

🏟️
MYAS / MoSPI
Khelo India Scheme
The Khelo India Scheme promotes grassroots sports infrastructure including multi-purpose indoor halls, stadiums, swimming pools, courts, football grounds and sports academies. It explicitly recognizes convergence with CSR funds, corporate/community sponsorships and District Mineral Funds — making sports complexes on mine land eligible for blended financing.
⛏️
MoC / CCO
Mine Closure Guidelines 2025
The Ministry of Coal's 2025 mine-closure guidelines emphasize scientific, systematic closure planning and community-oriented repurposing of mined-out land. Sports and recreation complexes on stabilized, reclaimed mine land are a recognized post-mining land-use pathway consistent with these guidelines.
💰
MoC / DMs
District Mineral Foundation (DMF)
The DMF / PMKKKY scheme mandates spending in mining-affected areas on community welfare, infrastructure and youth development. Sports complexes on mine land directly serve affected communities and are eligible for DMF convergence funding alongside CSR and government scheme grants.
🌿
CCO
A.R.T.H.A. — CCO Green Finance
The A.R.T.H.A. framework from the Coal Controller Organisation provides guidance on green finance mechanisms for mine repurposing, including blended finance, ESG financing, social impact bonds and community-benefit obligations — all applicable to sports and recreation complex development.
🤝
MCA / MoC
CSR / ESG for Sports Infrastructure
Schedule VII of the Companies Act 2013 (as amended) recognizes sports promotion, youth development and community health as eligible CSR activities. Coal sector companies with large CSR obligations — such as CIL subsidiaries — can direct funds to sports complex development on mine land as a visible welfare investment.
📋
CCO
L.I.V.E.S. & R.E.C.L.A.I.M. Frameworks
The L.I.V.E.S. and R.E.C.L.A.I.M. mine-closure handbooks from the Coal Controller Organisation identify community-oriented, productive, welfare-generating land reuse as the priority for mine closure planning. Sports complexes are among the explicitly recognized community infrastructure pathways.
🏦
NITI / MoF
VGF / Blended Finance for PPP
The Government of India's Viability Gap Funding (VGF) mechanism supports PPP projects in social and community infrastructure. Large sports complex projects on mine land under DBFOT or Concession models can leverage VGF alongside CSR and DMF contributions for financial viability.

PROJECT VISION

Project concept, strategic rationale, site suitability assessment and indicative scale matrix for sports complex development on closed coal mine land.

This concept proposes the development of Sports Complex Zones on closed, reclaimed, stabilized, or repurposed coal mine land. The objective is to convert under-utilized mine land into a community-oriented, youth-focused, health-promoting and income-supporting land-use system centered around outdoor sports grounds, indoor sports halls, athletics tracks, multi-purpose courts, fitness zones, training academies, community recreation spaces and sports-linked livelihood activities.

Key Benefits:

  • Converts closed mine land into a productive community asset
  • Supports youth engagement, fitness, discipline and sports talent development
  • Provides inclusive facilities for children, women, youth, workers, senior citizens and persons with disabilities
  • Creates local jobs in coaching, operations, security, maintenance, events, landscaping and facility management
  • Can be integrated with eco-parks, mine-tourism, water bodies, cycling tracks, walking trails and plantation zones
  • Supports CSR, ESG and just-transition objectives
  • Reduces risk of abandoned land misuse, encroachment or unsafe public access
  • Can generate revenue through memberships, tournaments, training academies, rentals, kiosks, events and sponsorships
  • Can be developed in phases depending on land availability and demand
  • Enhances the social image of mine-closure projects by creating a visible public-good asset

This aligns with India's mine-closure shift toward productive, community-oriented repurposing as reflected in L.I.V.E.S., R.E.C.L.A.I.M. and A.R.T.H.A. frameworks. The Khelo India Scheme explicitly recognizes convergence with CSR funds, public/private/community sponsorships and District Mineral Funds, making sports complexes on mine land eligible for blended financing.

STRATEGIC STRUCTURING

Comparative assessment of key models, model-wise responsibility logic, and combined revenue and livelihood comparison for sports complex procurement.

The following section identifies the key owner-side preparatory actions that should ideally be completed before issuing the first Request for Proposal. These actions are aimed at reducing uncertainty for prospective bidders, improving project bankability, clarifying site availability, identifying approval pathways and risks, and defining the technical and commercial scope of the project. Completing these preparatory steps before tendering can improve bid quality, attract credible responses, and reduce the risk of delays during project implementation.

Before issuing an RFP for a Sports, Recreation and Community Fitness Landscape on closed / reclaimed mine land, the mine owner should prepare the following in priority order:

  1. Land and mine-closure confirmation — Confirm land ownership, closure status, mine-closure linkage and permissibility of sports, recreation, fitness and community-use facilities.
  2. Legal and land-use screening — Review land-use restrictions, public-access rights, lease / concession rights, user charges, revenue sharing, advertising rights and liability provisions.
  3. Safety and no-go zone mapping — Identify safe and unsafe areas, including unstable dumps, highwalls, subsidence-prone areas, unsafe water bodies, active mine interfaces and restricted infrastructure.
  4. Preliminary technical site assessment — Prepare basic topographic survey, geotechnical screening, slope-stability review, drainage assessment and ground-bearing suitability for playfields, courts, buildings and parking.
  5. Environmental and public-health screening — Assess soil condition, dust, residual contamination, waterlogging, noise, ecological sensitivity and suitability for children, youth, women, senior citizens and public users.
  6. Sports demand and facility suitability assessment — Identify suitable facilities such as open gym, walking track, football ground, kabaddi court, volleyball court, basketball court, badminton hall, cricket practice nets, athletics track or indoor multipurpose hall based on local demand and site capacity.
  7. Community and stakeholder consultation — Consult panchayats, schools, colleges, youth clubs, women groups, district sports office, local administration, health department and nearby communities.
  8. Preliminary zoning and access plan — Prepare a basic layout showing playfields, courts, indoor hall, open gym, walking track, parking, toilets, changing rooms, green buffers, emergency access and no-go areas.
  9. Inclusivity and basic public amenities plan — Provide for separate toilets, drinking water, changing rooms and access for men, women and persons with disabilities, consistent with Khelo India infrastructure expectations and universal-access principles.
  10. Fire, life-safety and crowd-safety screening — Identify fire access, emergency exits, assembly areas, lighting, CCTV, first-aid room, public-address system and crowd-management needs, especially for indoor halls and spectator areas. The National Building Code Part 4 covers fire prevention, life safety and fire-protection requirements for buildings.
  11. Market, revenue and O&M assessment — Estimate demand and possible revenue from memberships, court booking, coaching, tournaments, events, kiosks, parking, advertisements and fitness programmes.
  12. Cost estimate and model selection — Prepare broad CAPEX and 5-year O&M estimates and identify the preferred model such as Hybrid, Concession / LDOT, DBFOT, EPC + O&M or Management Contract.
  13. Draft RFP scope and service obligations — Prepare bidder scope covering design, construction, sports equipment, O&M, safety, coaching support, local employment, community access, women-friendly use and maintenance standards.
  14. Bid evaluation framework — Define evaluation criteria covering sports-infrastructure experience, technical capacity, O&M plan, safety plan, financial capacity, community integration, revenue proposal and maintenance commitment.

The following section lists the principal activities that may be undertaken by the selected contractor, developer, concessionaire, operator, or implementation agency after award of the project. These activities generally include detailed site investigations, engineering design, statutory submissions and approvals, equipment procurement, construction, commissioning, operational planning, staff training, and performance monitoring. The distinction between pre-RFP owner preparedness and post-award implementation responsibilities helps ensure an appropriate and efficient allocation of effort across the project development cycle.

After award, the selected concessionaire / contractor / O&M agency / facility-management operator can take up the following activities in chronological order:

  1. Detailed site investigation — Undertake detailed topographic survey, geotechnical investigation, soil testing, drainage study, utility mapping and final safety-zone verification.
  2. Detailed Project Report and final master plan — Prepare the DPR with final sports master plan, facility mix, layout, cost estimate, implementation schedule, O&M plan, revenue model and statutory approval plan.
  3. Detailed engineering and sports-field design — Prepare designs for playfields, courts, indoor hall, walking track, open gym, seating, lighting, fencing, parking, utilities, stormwater drainage and landscape buffers.
  4. Compliance and approval planning — Obtain required building permissions, fire-safety approvals, electrical safety clearances, local-body permissions, accessibility compliance and other statutory approvals before execution.
  5. Procurement of sports equipment and systems — Procure sports equipment, fitness equipment, turf / flooring material, lighting, CCTV, PA system, access-control system, first-aid equipment and maintenance tools.
  6. Construction and installation — Develop grounds, courts, indoor facilities, toilets, changing rooms, drinking-water points, pathways, parking, fencing, green buffers, kiosks and visitor amenities.
  7. Preparation of operating procedures — Prepare SOPs for booking, user access, coaching, events, crowd control, equipment use, emergency response, maintenance, cleaning and incident reporting.
  8. Recruitment and training — Recruit and train facility managers, coaches, ground staff, security staff, cleaning staff, first-aid personnel, event staff and local youth for sports-facility operations.
  9. Community-use and livelihood integration — Operationalize school-use hours, women-friendly slots, youth coaching, local tournaments, SHG kiosks, equipment rental, food stalls and local maintenance contracts.
  10. Trial operation and safety audit — Conduct trial use, lighting checks, fire-safety checks, crowd-flow checks, equipment testing, accessibility review and third-party safety audit before public opening.
  11. Branding and programme linkage — Link the facility with Khelo India, Fit India, district sports programmes, school competitions, youth leagues, community fitness drives and CSR / ESG outreach. Khelo India is designed to revive sports culture at the grassroots level, while Fit India promotes physical activity and fitness in daily life.
  12. Commissioning and public opening — Open the facility after completion of safety checks, staff deployment, operating systems, public amenities and mine-owner approval.
  13. Regular O&M and monitoring — Carry out routine maintenance, turf care, court resurfacing, equipment inspection, cleaning, safety checks, revenue reporting, footfall monitoring and grievance handling.
  14. Phased expansion — Expand facilities such as indoor stadium, athletics track, swimming pool, academy, hostel, sports science room or event plaza based on demand, revenue, safety record and owner approval.

MONITORING GUIDANCE

Pre-RFP owner readiness checklist, post-award activities, 15-step implementation pathway, and preferred models for sports complex development on closed coal mine land.

The following way forward summarizes the preferred development approach for the proposed repurposing option, based on the assessment presented in PRISM (Post Mining Repurposing Implementation and Strategy Models). It identifies the most suitable implementation models in order of preference, highlights key technical and due-diligence considerations, and outlines the recommended next steps and broad development logic. This section is intended to guide internal decision-making and support the project owner in progressing from concept-stage evaluation toward feasibility assessment, project structuring, regulatory approvals, and implementation planning.

  • Hybrid Model — preferred because sports complexes must balance public access, youth development, academy operation and revenue services. The hybrid mix should include owner-funded core facilities + private academy / operator + CSR / government support + local coaching and employment obligations.
  • Concession / Lease-Develop-Operate — strong for long-term community-linked sports complexes with mandated access, youth programming, revenue sharing and local employment obligations.
  • DBFOT — suitable for large structured sports parks needing private finance, professional management, indoor stadium, athletics track, academy, hostel and event facilities.

Secondary option: EPC + O&M is suitable for welfare-driven, owner-controlled sports complexes where community access, youth training and maintenance must remain tightly managed.

Technical priorities: Land stability, drainage, lighting, safety, toilets, changing rooms, parking, accessibility, security, coach availability and O&M cost must be assessed before procurement. Khelo India recognizes facilities such as multipurpose indoor halls, stadiums, swimming pools, courts, football grounds and sports academies, which can be selectively adapted to mine land after safety screening.

Recommended development logic: Start with walking track, open gym, football / multipurpose field, courts, indoor hall, toilets and coaching programmes. Later add athletics track, indoor stadium, academy, hostel or swimming pool only if demand and O&M capacity exist.
