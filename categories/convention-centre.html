Final Recommendations / Convention Centre
Convention Centre
Global Precedents
Dolní Vítkovice Convention Centre, Ostrava, Czech Republic

Convention centres are purpose-built facilities designed to host large-scale events such as conferences, exhibitions, trade shows, and business gatherings, playing a key role in promoting economic activity and knowledge exchange. In the context of post-mining landscapes, reclaimed land can be effectively utilized to develop convention centres by transforming underutilized areas into vibrant hubs for business tourism and community events, supported by available land and potential infrastructure, thereby contributing to regional development and diversification.

The establishment of convention centres in such areas can generate significant employment and business opportunities for local communities, particularly those transitioning from mining-based livelihoods, by creating roles in event management, hospitality, logistics, and support services. However, successful implementation requires addressing key challenges, including developing high-quality infrastructure and connectivity, ensuring sustainable operations, attracting consistent event traffic, and building strong institutional and commercial partnerships to ensure long-term viability and economic success.

PROJECT VISION

Transforming closed mine land and associated assets into a business-supporting, tourism-enabling, event-oriented, and community-serving public facility that contributes to sustainable mine closure and regional economic diversification.

This concept proposes the development of a Convention Centre within a closed, reclaimed, or repurposed mine area as part of a broader post-mining land-use strategy. The objective is to transform under-utilized mine land and associated assets into a business-supporting, tourism-enabling, event-oriented, and community-serving public facility.

The proposed Convention Centre is intended as an early-stage concept and structuring framework to support internal decision-making, stakeholder consultation, feasibility planning, approval mapping, and identification of an appropriate development and operating model. It is not a DPR, tender document, or final engineering proposal.

A Convention Centre can be a strong repurposing option where a mine area has:

  • Proximity to urban or industrial growth centres
  • Access to major roads, rail, or airports
  • Potential for business tourism, institutional gatherings, exhibitions, or trade events
  • Availability of sizeable land for integrated development
  • Opportunity to anchor destination-led post-mining redevelopment

Unlike purely passive land reuse, a Convention Centre can serve as an economic activation asset, enabling conferences, exhibitions, trade fairs, training events, business meetings, public gatherings, and tourism-linked activity.

  • Generation of local employment in construction, operations, event management, housekeeping, security, catering, maintenance, and logistics

Hybrid Model

The Hybrid model combines public funding / ownership elements with private participation in fit-outs, operations, hospitality, event management, or revenue components. It is particularly suitable where the project has both public and commercial objectives and allows explicit provisions for local enterprises, vendors, hospitality, and community-based event enterprises.

STRATEGIC STRUCTURING

Pre-RFP readiness requirements, post-award scope, and the indicative 13-step implementation pathway for Convention Centre development in a mine area.

The following section identifies the key owner-side preparatory actions that should ideally be completed before issuing the first Request for Proposal. These actions are aimed at reducing uncertainty for prospective bidders, improving project bankability, clarifying site availability, identifying approval pathways and risks, and defining the technical and commercial scope of the project. Completing these preparatory steps before tendering can improve bid quality, attract credible responses, and reduce the risk of delays during project implementation.

Before issuing the first RFP for a Convention Centre in a repurposed mine area, the mine owner should prepare the following in priority order:

  1. Land title and mine-closure linkage — Confirm ownership, access, mine-closure status, approved post-mining land-use position, and whether convention / public-assembly / commercial visitor use is permissible on the identified land.
  2. Land-use and legal permissibility screening — Review zoning, Change of Land Use (CLU) requirement if applicable, development-control restrictions, concession rights, lease conditions, commercial-use permissibility, and owner's right to levy user charges, rentals, and revenue share.
  3. Safe developable-area mapping — Identify safe and excluded zones, including unstable slopes, subsidence-prone areas, restricted mine infrastructure, hazardous voids, unsafe water edges, utility corridors, and no-development buffers.
  4. Preliminary technical site assessment — Prepare topographic survey, geotechnical screening, drainage assessment, access-road review, broad traffic feasibility, and buildability note for large-span halls, parking, and service areas.
  5. Preliminary planning and space programme — Define the broad project brief covering plenary hall, conference rooms, exhibition space, foyers, banquet / catering areas, back-of-house, parking, drop-off, logistics movement, and utility zones.
  6. Market and MICE demand assessment — Assess likely demand from government events, institutional meetings, trade bodies, exhibitions, conferences, training programmes, and business-tourism activity in line with MICE positioning.
  7. Accessibility and public-building compliance concept — Prepare a preliminary compliance note on barrier-free access, circulation, toilets, lifts, ramps, and public-use planning consistent with NBC 2016, Model Building Bye-Laws, and accessibility guidance.
  8. Fire and life-safety pathway note — Prepare a preliminary Fire NOC and life-safety strategy covering occupancy type, exit logic, fire tender access, refuge / assembly areas, alarm and suppression concept, and emergency evacuation approach.
  9. Utility and services feasibility — Assess availability of power, water, sewerage / STP, HVAC feasibility, digital connectivity, AV backbone, DG backup, CCTV, and public-address / building-management requirements.
  10. Approval mapping and statutory responsibility matrix — Identify the likely approval chain, including building plan sanction, fire approval, electrical permissions, occupancy / completion requirements, local-body approvals, and any tourism / hospitality interface approvals if relevant.
  11. Commercial and implementation-model assessment — Prepare broad CAPEX, 5-year O&M estimate, revenue logic, and preferred model selection such as Hybrid, Concession / Lease-Operate-Develop, DBFOT, or EPC + O&M / Facility Management.
  12. Draft RFP scope and bid framework — Finalize bidder scope covering design, construction, fit-out, operations, booking systems, event management support, maintenance, local service integration, and statutory compliance, along with bid-evaluation criteria for technical capacity, convention / venue experience, financial strength, O&M approach, and revenue proposal.

The following section lists the principal activities that may be undertaken by the selected contractor, developer, concessionaire, operator, or implementation agency after award of the project. These activities generally include detailed site investigations, engineering design, statutory submissions and approvals, equipment procurement, construction, commissioning, operational planning, staff training, and performance monitoring. The distinction between pre-RFP owner preparedness and post-award implementation responsibilities helps ensure an appropriate and efficient allocation of effort across the project development cycle.

After award, the selected concessionaire / contractor / operator / facility-management agency may take up the following activities in chronological order:

  1. Detailed surveys and investigations — Undertake detailed topographic survey, geotechnical investigation, traffic-impact inputs, drainage study, utility mapping, and foundation suitability assessment.
  2. Detailed master planning and layout finalization — Prepare the final site plan showing convention halls, meeting rooms, exhibition areas, banquet support, foyers, parking, drop-off, service entry, fire tender movement, utility zones, and pedestrian circulation.
  3. Detailed architectural, structural, and MEP design — Prepare detailed building design, large-span structural systems, façade, HVAC, electrical, plumbing, STP, fire-fighting, lifts, and integrated MEP coordination.
  4. Detailed AV, ICT, and event-systems design — Finalize AV, acoustics, interpretation systems if required, control room, digital signage, CCTV, Wi-Fi, access control, ticketing / booking systems, and conference technology integration.
  5. Detailed Project Report (DPR) — Prepare the DPR including detailed design basis, cost estimate, implementation schedule, utility design, code-compliance matrix, O&M plan, business plan, and phasing proposal, for owner approval.
  6. Statutory approvals and certifications — Obtain building plan approval, fire approvals / Fire NOC, electrical approvals, local-body permissions, environmental / utility consents as applicable, and pre-commissioning compliance clearances.
  7. Interior fit-out and functional infrastructure — Execute seating, partitions, exhibition systems, foyer treatment, kitchen / banquet fit-out, toilets, signage, business lounge areas, back-of-house spaces, and operator-specific functional installations.
  8. Procurement and installation of systems — Procure and install HVAC equipment, AV systems, lighting, CCTV, access control, public-address systems, DG / backup systems, ticketing and booking software, and facility-management systems.
  9. Operating procedures and facility-management planning — Prepare SOPs for event booking, crowd handling, emergency response, housekeeping, engineering maintenance, parking management, waste management, security, and vendor coordination.
  10. Recruitment and training — Recruit and train facility managers, technicians, event staff, housekeeping teams, security personnel, front-office staff, parking teams, and local support staff.
  11. Commercial activation and partnerships — Onboard catering partners, event managers, exhibition operators, technology vendors, local transport links, hospitality partners, and other service providers required for venue activation.
  12. Trial operations and systems testing — Conduct dry runs, mock events, HVAC and AV testing, fire and evacuation drills, integrated systems testing, and defect rectification before opening.
  13. Commissioning and public opening — Commission the Convention Centre after successful testing, staff readiness, statutory compliance, and operational preparedness.
  14. Regular operations and performance monitoring — Carry out routine O&M, booking management, preventive maintenance, safety checks, utility monitoring, event servicing, compliance reporting, and customer-service review.
  15. Phased expansion and optimization — Expand halls, exhibition areas, parking, hospitality interfaces, or technology systems in phases based on utilization, demand, revenue performance, and owner approval.

MONITORING GUIDANCE

Assessment of local livelihood generation potential, combined revenue and profitability comparison, and the strategic way forward for model selection and Convention Centre activation.

The following way forward summarizes the preferred development approach for the proposed repurposing option, based on the assessment presented in PRISM (Post Mining Repurposing Implementation and Strategy Models). It identifies the most suitable implementation models in order of preference, highlights key technical and due-diligence considerations, and outlines the recommended next steps and broad development logic. This section is intended to guide internal decision-making and support the project owner in progressing from concept-stage evaluation toward feasibility assessment, project structuring, regulatory approvals, and implementation planning.

Preferred Models, in order: (1) Hybrid Model, (2) Concession / Lease-Develop-Operate, (3) DBFOT, (4) EPC + O&M

Why preferred: Hybrid is preferred because convention centres need public-purpose use, professional event operation, tourism services and revenue management. The hybrid mix should include owner-funded core facility + private event operator + hospitality / F&B concession + local service participation.

Secondary option: EPC + O&M is suitable where the owner wants stronger control over public use, pricing and institutional events.

Suggestable technical care: Market demand, parking, access, fire safety, acoustics, HVAC, utilities, crowd management, logistics access and local traffic impact must be assessed.

Recommended development logic: Begin with a multipurpose hall for training, conferences, exhibitions and community events. Scale into banquet, exhibition, hospitality and business-tourism facilities only after demand is proven.
