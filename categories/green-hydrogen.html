P
PROJECT VISION

Project concept, strategic rationale, site suitability assessment and indicative scales for green hydrogen production & storage on closed coal mine land.

This concept proposes the development of a Green Hydrogen Production & Storage Facility on closed, reclaimed, or repurposed coal mine land as part of a sustainable mine-closure and post-mining land-use strategy.

The objective is to convert under-utilised mine land into a clean energy production, storage, training, and industrial decarbonization asset using renewable electricity and water electrolysis to produce hydrogen, followed by compression, storage, distribution, or conversion into downstream products.

A Green Hydrogen Production & Storage facility may include:

  • Renewable power supply from solar, wind, hybrid renewable energy, or grid-connected renewable energy
  • Water treatment and demineralization plant
  • Electrolyser system (alkaline, PEM, or SOEC)
  • Hydrogen purification, drying, and compression system
  • Hydrogen storage system (low-pressure buffer to high-pressure cascade)
  • Oxygen handling or utilization system
  • Hydrogen dispensing or loading station
  • Safety, fire, leak detection, ventilation, and emergency systems
  • SCADA, metering, and digital monitoring system
  • Training and skill development facility
  • Downstream linkage with industry, transport, blending, fertilizer, refinery, steel, or energy users

This concept aligns with India's National Green Hydrogen Mission (NGHM), launched in January 2023 with an outlay of ₹19,744 crore and a target to develop at least 5 million metric tonnes per annum of green hydrogen production capacity by 2030. India's Green Hydrogen Standard defines green hydrogen as hydrogen with well-to-gate emissions not exceeding 2 kg CO₂eq per kg H₂, averaged over 12 months.

Closed coal mine land can support this transition by providing large land parcels, existing power corridors, water-management infrastructure, roads, workshops, security systems, and a local workforce that can be reskilled for clean-energy employment.

Green Hydrogen Production & Storage is particularly suitable for selected closed coal mine areas where land, renewable-energy potential, water availability, grid connectivity, industrial offtake, and safety buffers can be established.

  • Converts post-mining land into a clean-energy production asset
  • Supports India's energy transition and decarbonization goals under the NGHM
  • Creates long-term skilled employment in renewable energy, electrolysis, mechanical systems, instrumentation, safety, and plant operation
  • Enables just transition for coal-dependent communities
  • Can reuse mine land, roads, substations, workshops, water reservoirs, administrative buildings, and security infrastructure
  • Supports green industrial development and downstream clean-fuel applications
  • Can integrate with solar parks, pumped storage, battery storage, industrial hubs, logistics parks, or green mobility corridors
  • Creates revenue through hydrogen sale, oxygen sale, storage charges, dispensing charges, renewable-energy services, carbon/ESG value, and training services
  • Supports domestic and export-oriented clean-energy value chains
  • Can be developed in modular phases, beginning with pilot-scale electrolysers and expanding to commercial-scale production

The National Green Hydrogen Mission recognizes the need for hydrogen supply chains, including pipelines, tankers, intermediate storage facilities, and last-mile distribution networks. Globally, the IEA notes that installed water electrolyser capacity reached 1.4 GW by the end of 2023, while announced projects could reach much higher capacity by 2030. Green hydrogen projects are technically promising but commercially sensitive — mine-repurposed projects should be developed only after confirming renewable power availability, water source, offtake, safety zone, and bankability.

STRATEGIC STRUCTURING

Pre-RFP readiness requirements and post-award actions for green hydrogen production & storage projects on closed mine land.

The following section identifies the key owner-side preparatory actions that should ideally be completed before issuing the first Request for Proposal. These actions are aimed at reducing uncertainty for prospective bidders, improving project bankability, clarifying site availability, identifying approval pathways and risks, and defining the technical and commercial scope of the project. Completing these preparatory steps before tendering can improve bid quality, attract credible responses, and reduce the risk of delays during project implementation.

Before issuing an RFP for a Green Hydrogen Production & Storage-based mine repurposing project, the mine owner should prepare the following in priority order:

  1. Land and mine-closure confirmation — Confirm land ownership, mine-closure status, post-mining land-use permissibility, and linkage of the proposed green hydrogen facility with the approved mine closure / repurposing plan.
  2. Legal, policy and concession screening — Review land-leasing rights, commercial hydrogen production rights, concession structure, revenue sharing, electricity procurement, water use, liability, insurance, and applicable Indian regulatory requirements.
  3. Safe and no-go zone mapping — Identify safe and unsafe zones, including unstable dumps, highwalls, subsidence-prone areas, abandoned galleries, restricted infrastructure, water bodies, and buffer areas required for hydrogen production and storage.
  4. Preliminary technical site assessment — Prepare topographic survey, geotechnical screening, bearing-capacity assessment, drainage review, access-road assessment, and preliminary layout suitability for electrolyser, compressor, storage, utilities, and emergency access.
  5. Renewable energy and grid-readiness assessment — Assess captive solar / hybrid renewable potential, open-access renewable power, substation capacity, grid connectivity, power quality, backup power, and alignment with the National Green Hydrogen Mission objective of renewable-energy-based hydrogen production.
  6. Water availability and treatment assessment — Confirm raw-water source, seasonal reliability, competing uses, water quality, demineralized-water requirement, wastewater handling, and statutory permissions for water abstraction or reuse.
  7. Green hydrogen standard and certification readiness — Prepare a preliminary emissions-accounting, metering, renewable-energy matching, documentation, and monitoring approach in line with India's Green Hydrogen Standard and the Green Hydrogen Certification Scheme of India.
  8. Technology and storage option assessment — Identify suitable electrolyser technology, hydrogen purification, compression pressure, storage mode, tube-trailer loading, dispensing requirement, oxygen handling, and future scalability.
  9. Safety, statutory and hazard-screening framework — Prepare preliminary HAZID, hazardous-area classification, fire-safety note, leak-detection approach, emergency-response concept, and check applicability of PESO requirements under the Gas Cylinders Rules / hydrogen storage provisions.
  10. Offtake and market assessment — Identify potential hydrogen users such as refineries, steel units, fertilizer plants, industrial clusters, mobility users, gas utilities, hydrogen hubs, research institutions, or pilot transport projects.
  11. Cost, phasing and implementation model selection — Prepare broad CAPEX, 5-year O&M estimate, unit production-cost assumptions, phasing plan, and select a preferred model such as Hybrid, Concession / Lease-Develop-Operate, DBFOT, BOOT, or EPC + O&M.
  12. Draft RFP scope, obligations and bid evaluation framework — Prepare bidder scope covering design, finance, construction, renewable power sourcing, certification, safety compliance, O&M, local skilling, emergency response, reporting, and evaluation criteria for technical capability, hydrogen experience, safety record, financial capacity, offtake plan, and revenue proposal.

The following section lists the principal activities that may be undertaken by the selected contractor, developer, concessionaire, operator, or implementation agency after award of the project. These activities generally include detailed site investigations, engineering design, statutory submissions and approvals, equipment procurement, construction, commissioning, operational planning, staff training, and performance monitoring. The distinction between pre-RFP owner preparedness and post-award implementation responsibilities helps ensure an appropriate and efficient allocation of effort across the project development cycle.

After award, the selected concessionaire / contractor / O&M agency / green hydrogen developer can take up the following activities in chronological order:

  1. Detailed site investigation — Undertake detailed topographic survey, drone mapping, geotechnical investigation, foundation investigation, drainage study, safe-zone verification, and final plant-area demarcation.
  2. Detailed feasibility and DPR preparation — Prepare the detailed DPR covering final capacity, renewable-energy source, water system, electrolyser technology, compression, storage, safety systems, financial model, approvals plan, O&M plan, and implementation schedule.
  3. Final master planning and plant layout — Prepare the final layout showing electrolyser block, water-treatment plant, electrical yard, compressor area, hydrogen storage, tube-trailer / dispensing area, oxygen handling, control room, utilities, safety buffers, and emergency routes.
  4. Approvals, licences and statutory clearances — Obtain applicable approvals for land use, electricity procurement, water use, environmental compliance, factory / labour requirements, PESO-related hydrogen storage permissions, and any dispensing or compressed-gas requirements.
  5. Renewable power and water tie-up — Finalize captive renewable project, open-access renewable power, PPA / energy-banking arrangement, grid interconnection, water source, and demineralized-water treatment scheme.
  6. Detailed engineering and safety studies — Prepare detailed engineering, HAZOP, QRA, hazardous-area classification, fire and gas detection design, emergency shutdown logic, ventilation plan, earthing, lightning protection, and hydrogen safety management system.
  7. Procurement of major equipment — Procure electrolyser package, rectifier / power electronics, water-treatment system, hydrogen purification unit, compressor, storage system, safety valves, sensors, SCADA, fire-safety system, and certified pressure equipment.
  8. Construction and installation — Construct foundations, roads, drainage, electrical systems, control room, pipe racks, storage foundations, utility systems, and install electrolysers, compressors, storage vessels / cascades, metering, and safety systems.
  9. Certification, metering and digital monitoring setup — Install metering, data logging, renewable-energy accounting, emissions-monitoring, SCADA, leak detection, audit trails, and documentation systems required for green hydrogen certification and operational reporting.
  10. Recruitment, training and emergency preparedness — Recruit and train operators, technicians, safety staff, local youth, fire-response teams, and maintenance personnel in hydrogen safety, PPE, SOPs, emergency shutdown, leak response, and first response.
  11. Testing, commissioning and safety validation — Conduct pre-commissioning checks, pressure testing, leak testing, interlock testing, trial electrolysis, compressor trials, storage validation, mock drills, third-party safety audit, and performance guarantee tests.
  12. Offtake finalization and commercial supply readiness — Finalize hydrogen sale agreements, oxygen utilization, tube-trailer logistics, dispensing protocols, billing systems, customer interface, product quality specifications, and delivery schedule.
  13. Commercial operation and compliance reporting — Commence commercial operations after safety clearance and performance validation, with regular reporting on hydrogen production, renewable-energy use, water consumption, emissions intensity, incidents, and O&M performance.
  14. Monitoring, audit and phased expansion — Undertake periodic safety audits, certification audits, equipment inspection, environmental monitoring, community-safety review, and phased expansion based on offtake, safety record, certification status, and mine-owner approval.

The following way forward summarizes the preferred development approach for the proposed repurposing option, based on the assessment presented in PRISM (Post Mining Repurposing Implementation and Strategy Models). It identifies the most suitable implementation models in order of preference, highlights key technical and due-diligence considerations, and outlines the recommended next steps and broad development logic. This section is intended to guide internal decision-making and support the project owner in progressing from concept-stage evaluation toward feasibility assessment, project structuring, regulatory approvals, and implementation planning.

For Green Hydrogen Production & Storage-based mine repurposing, the most practical approach is:

Preferred Models:

  1. Hybrid Model — most suitable for balancing mine closure, clean energy, livelihood, safety, commercial viability, and ESG value
  2. Concession / Lease-Develop-Operate — very suitable where mine owner retains land while specialist partner manages technology and market risk
  3. DBFOT — suitable for large structured PPP hydrogen hubs requiring private finance and professional operation

Secondary Option: EPC + O&M / Facility Management for pilot or demonstration projects where the mine owner wants stronger control over safety, learning, and technology development.

Suggested Facility Positioning: For closed coal mine land, the facility should preferably be positioned as a Green Hydrogen Production, Storage and Clean Energy Transition Hub with renewable-energy supply, water treatment, electrolyser block, hydrogen purification and drying, compression, buffer and high-pressure storage cascade, tube-trailer loading, oxygen handling, safety and emergency control, SCADA, green hydrogen certification, training and skill development centre, local employment programme, research / demonstration zone, and future expansion space for ammonia, methanol, mobility, or industrial offtake.

The facility should be developed in a phased and demand-linked manner. A pilot project may be suitable for learning and capacity building, while larger hydrogen hubs should be pursued only where renewable power, water security, safety clearances, certification, offtake, and financing are clearly established.
