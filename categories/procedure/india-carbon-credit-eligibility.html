Reg. 01 / Basics

What is a carbon credit, really?

Before assessing eligibility, it helps to know what you're actually applying for.

A carbon credit is a certificate representing one tonne of carbon dioxide equivalent (tCO₂e) that has been reduced, removed, or avoided by a specific project — a solar plant, a biogas unit, a mangrove restoration, a landfill-gas capture system — and independently verified by an accredited third party.

Credits are only issued after a project proves that the reduction is real, measurable, additional (it would not have happened anyway) and not already claimed elsewhere. Once issued, a credit is a serialised, registry-tracked unit that can be sold, banked, or retired against a climate claim.

In India, two systems currently exist side by side, and a project developer typically chooses one — or occasionally both, with safeguards against double counting.

Domestic — CCTS Offset Mechanism

Run by the Bureau of Energy Efficiency (BEE) under India's Carbon Credit Trading Scheme, 2023. Open to any non-obligated entity — a company, NGO, farmer group, or local body — for approved project types. Certificates (CCCs) trade on Indian power exchanges.

International — Verra VCS (and similar standards)

The world's most widely used voluntary carbon standard. Projects follow an approved VCS methodology, are validated and verified by an independent body, and earn Verified Carbon Units (VCUs) tradable to global buyers.

Not the same as the Green Credit Programme

India's separate Green Credit Programme (under the Environment Protection Act) rewards broader environmental actions. It is not a carbon credit and generally cannot be stacked with a CCTS-registered project without specific BEE approval.

Reg. 02 / Eligibility

Who is eligible to earn carbon credits

Requirements differ slightly by standard, but the underlying tests are consistent across Verra VCS and the CCTS Offset Mechanism.

✓ Almost any entity type

Companies, NGOs, cooperatives, farmer groups, local governments, and individual project developers can all register a project. There is no requirement to be a large corporate.

✓ New, not business-as-usual

The activity generally must not have already started, and must go beyond what would have happened anyway. Projects mandated by existing law are not eligible.

✓ Additionality

You must show the emission reduction depends on carbon revenue — a project that is already commercially viable without it will struggle to qualify.

✓ Approved methodology

The project must follow a currently approved methodology — a Verra VCS methodology, or one of BEE's approved CCTS offset methodologies — that sets out how to calculate, monitor and verify reductions.

✓ Legal right to the project and the credits

You need clear legal rights to carry out the activity (land, licence, equipment ownership) and to be issued the resulting credits — usually through title, lease, or a documented agreement.

✓ No double counting

The same emission reduction cannot be claimed under more than one scheme, and — for credits sold internationally — India must apply a "corresponding adjustment" under the Paris Agreement.

Sectors currently covered by approved methodologies include:

Renewable energy (solar, wind, hydro) Renewable energy + storage Offshore wind Green hydrogen Industrial energy efficiency Landfill methane recovery Compressed biogas Mangrove afforestation / reforestation Forestry & land use (AFOLU) Waste management
Reg. 03 / Pathway

How a project moves from idea to issued credit

The same broad pathway applies whether you register under Verra VCS or the CCTS Offset Mechanism — only the registry, verifier pool, and certificate name differ.

Reg. 04 / Procedure

Step-by-step registration procedure

A closer look at each stage, combining Verra VCS practice with BEE's Offset Mechanism procedure.

01

Confirm the activity and pick a pathway

Decide whether your project fits an approved Verra VCS methodology, a BEE-approved CCTS offset methodology, or both. Check the project has not already started, and that its sector and scale are covered by an existing methodology.

02

Establish additionality and the baseline

Show why the project would not happen without carbon revenue, and calculate the "baseline" emissions it will improve on. This is the single most scrutinised part of any application.

03

Prepare the Project Design Document (PDD)

Document the technology, ownership, methodology application, monitoring plan, and expected emission reductions in the standard's required PDD template.

04

Register the entity and the project

For CCTS: register as a non-obligated entity and list the project on the Indian Carbon Market (ICM) Registry operated by the Grid Controller of India. For Verra: create a registry account and submit the project for listing.

05

Independent validation

An Accredited Carbon Verification Agency (ACVA) or a Verra-approved Validation/Verification Body (VVB) reviews the PDD, visits the site, and confirms the project meets programme rules. Note: the body that validates cannot also verify the same project.

06

Implement and monitor

Run the project according to the approved monitoring plan — metering, record-keeping, and data quality assurance as specified in the methodology.

07

Verification and issuance

After a monitoring period, a second independent body verifies the actual emission reductions achieved. Once approved, the regulator (BEE, or Verra) issues serialised credits (CCCs or VCUs) into your registry account.

08

Trade, bank, or retire

Sell credits on Indian power exchanges (IEX/PXIL) or to voluntary buyers, hold them for future use, or retire them permanently against a stated climate claim.

Reg. 05 / Governing framework

Governing rules and regulations

The legal backbone of carbon credit eligibility in India, spanning domestic law and the international standard.

India — domestic framework

2022
Energy Conservation (Amendment) Act, 2022

Inserted Section 14AA into the Energy Conservation Act, 2001, giving the Central Government power to notify a carbon credit trading scheme.

28 Jun 2023 · S.O. 2825(E)
Carbon Credit Trading Scheme (CCTS), 2023

Establishes the Indian Carbon Market with a Compliance Mechanism for obligated (energy-intensive) entities and an Offset Mechanism for voluntary participants.

19 Dec 2023 · S.O. 5369(E)
Amendment adding the Offset Mechanism

Opened voluntary project registration to any non-obligated entity — companies, NGOs, farmer groups, local bodies.

Jul 2024 & Mar 2025
BEE Detailed Procedures (Compliance & Offset)

Operating rulebooks covering MRV, verification agency accreditation, methodology approval, and registry mechanics.

Ongoing
Institutional roles

BEE administers the scheme and accredits verifiers; Grid Controller of India (GCI) runs the registry; CERC regulates trading on power exchanges (IEX, PXIL); the National Steering Committee for the ICM sets policy.

Verra & international framework

VCS Standard & Program Guide
Core Verra rulebook

Sets out project eligibility, the sectoral scopes covered, and the registration and issuance process. Every project must apply an approved methodology and pass third-party validation and verification.

v5.0 · from Jan 2027
VCS Version 5.0 overhaul

A structural update introducing new programme principles and nine Verified Carbon Unit quality attributes. Projects already listed transition gradually; new project requests from 1 January 2027 follow v5 fully.

ICVCM
Core Carbon Principles (CCP)

An independent integrity benchmark layered on top of VCS. Verra's programme is "CCP-Eligible"; individual methodology categories separately earn "CCP-Approved" status for higher-quality labelling.

Paris Agreement
Article 6.2 & 6.4

Governs cross-border transfer of credits. Where credits generated in India are sold internationally, a "corresponding adjustment" prevents the reduction being counted twice — once by the buyer, once toward India's NDC.

Choosing between the two: the CCTS Offset Mechanism is built for the Indian market and domestic buyers; Verra VCS opens access to a larger international buyer pool, often at a price premium. Some developers pursue both, but the same tonne of CO₂e cannot be certified — and sold — twice.

Reg. 07 / Self-check

Quick eligibility self-check

Answer four questions for an indicative read on your project. This is a guide only — final eligibility is determined by BEE or Verra during formal review.

1. Has the project activity already started or been operating for some time?

2. Is the project legally required by an existing law or regulation?

3. Would the project go ahead anyway, without carbon credit revenue?

4. Does an approved methodology exist for your activity (Verra VCS or CCTS)?

Reg. 08 / Worth knowing

Common reasons applications get delayed or rejected

These come up repeatedly across both the Verra and CCTS pipelines — worth checking before you submit.

Weak additionality evidence

A project that is already profitable without carbon finance is the single most common reason for rejection. Build a clear financial or barrier-analysis case early.

Unclear legal rights

Disputed land title, missing lease documentation, or unclear ownership of emission-reducing equipment routinely stalls validation.

Double counting

Registering the same tonne of CO₂e under CCTS and another scheme (or claiming it internationally without a corresponding adjustment) risks credit cancellation.

Outdated methodology version

Methodologies are periodically revised — Verra's v4→v5 transition is a current example. Confirm you're using the version in force for your submission date.

Same verifier for validation and verification

Independence rules require different accredited bodies for these two steps. Using the same ACVA/VVB for both is not permitted.

Incomplete monitoring data

Missing calibration records, gaps in metering data, or informal record-keeping are frequent causes of verification delay.

Reg. 09 / Contacts

Who to contact

Start with the body that administers the pathway you've chosen.

Scheme administrator

Bureau of Energy Efficiency (BEE)

Administers the CCTS, accredits verification agencies, and manages the Offset Mechanism registration for non-obligated entities.

beeindia.gov.in
Registry operator

Grid Controller of India (GCI)

Operates the Indian Carbon Market (ICM) Registry that records issuance, transfer, ownership, and retirement of Carbon Credit Certificates.

Market regulator

Central Electricity Regulatory Commission (CERC)

Regulates CCC trading and oversees the designated power exchanges (IEX, PXIL) where certificates are bought and sold.

Policy oversight

Ministry of Environment, Forest & Climate Change (MoEFCC)

Notifies GHG emission intensity targets and co-chairs the National Steering Committee for the Indian Carbon Market.

International standard

Verra

Owner and administrator of the Verified Carbon Standard (VCS) Program and its registry, methodologies, and rulebook.

verra.org
Independent review

Accredited verifiers (ACVAs / VVBs)

Third-party bodies accredited by BEE (for CCTS) or approved by Verra (for VCS) to validate and verify individual projects.
